This is a fantastic foundation for a portfolio. It already does the most important thing right: it focuses on **business value and solving real operational friction** rather than just listing coding languages. For a sales admin or sales operations role, hiring managers want to see systems thinking, organization, and efficiency.

Since the site is built on Next.js, you have a lot of flexibility to introduce modern, lightweight UI elements without compromising the professional, data-driven tone. Here are some components and design ideas to elevate the landing page:

### 1. The Hero Section: Show, Don't Just Tell

Right now, the hero is text-heavy. Sales operations is all about organizing chaos, so the first impression should be visually striking and incredibly organized.

- **Isometric Dashboard Mockup:** Include a high-quality, floating isometric graphic showing a clean Excel sheet fading or transforming into your web dashboard. It instantly communicates the "Excel-to-Web" transformation.
- **"Impact" Badges:** Add small, floating pill-shaped badges around the hero text with sample operational metrics (e.g., `⚡ Reduces manual entry by 40%`, `✓ 100% SKU Validation`, `📊 Automated Aging`).
- **Primary CTA Polish:** Give the `[Open Dashboard]` button a subtle pulse animation or a modern gradient border to draw the eye immediately.

### 2. Modernize the Workflows: The "Bento Box" Grid

A linear list of workflows is functional, but a **Bento Grid layout** (popularized by Apple and modern SaaS landing pages) is perfect for an admin portfolio because it inherently looks like a highly organized dashboard.

- Turn Workflows 01 through 04 into distinct cards in a grid.
- **Micro-interactions:** When a user hovers over the "Order Validation" card, display a subtle animation of a red error turning into a green checkmark. When they hover over "Payment Aging," show a tiny, animated bar chart sorting itself.
- This proves you understand modern UI (crucial for building internal tools) while keeping the focus on the administrative tasks.

### 3. Visual Systems Thinking: The Flow Diagram

The section _"One direction, start to finish"_ is currently text outlining the process. For a sales ops role, your ability to map a process is arguably your most marketable skill.

- **Animated Timeline/Flowchart:** Replace the text chain (`Orders Excel -> Order Validation -> etc.`) with a clean, horizontal timeline or a minimal flowchart.
- Libraries like `React Flow` or even simple CSS step-indicators can make this look like a professional consulting diagram.
- Highlight the "Happy Path" in one color (e.g., green or blue) and the "Exception Path" (Backorders) in a subtle warning color (yellow/orange) to show you anticipate edge cases.

### 4. "Before & After" Comparison Slider

Nothing sells an automated toolkit better than visualizing the pain it removes.

- Add a section with a visual slider (a UI component where you drag a handle left to right).
- **Left Side (The Problem):** A messy, generic spreadsheet with missing fields and highlighted errors ("Typical Manual Order Log").
- **Right Side (Your Solution):** Your clean, validated Next.js dashboard view.

### 5. Add a "My Operations Philosophy" Section

Hiring managers might look at a Python/FastAPI/Next.js stack and wonder, _"Is this person a software developer or a sales admin?"_ You need to actively bridge this gap.

- Add a short, text-focused section with a professional headshot.
- Include a brief mission statement that grounds your technical skills in administrative reality. For example: _"I build tools not just to write code, but to eliminate operational bottlenecks, ensure clean data entry, and give sales teams their time back."_

### 6. Subtle Design Tweaks for a SaaS Vibe

- **Typography:** Ensure you are using a clean, modern sans-serif font (like Inter, Geist, or Roboto).
- **Borders and Shadows:** Use very subtle borders (`border-gray-200` in Tailwind) and faint, diffused drop shadows to give the cards depth without looking cluttered.
- **Sticky Header:** Keep a minimal navigation bar pinned to the top of the screen as the user scrolls, allowing them to jump straight to the "Open Dashboard" button at any time.

By framing your technical capabilities (the Next.js stack) through the lens of extreme organization and process improvement (the Bento grids, flowcharts, and ROI metrics), the page will look incredibly sharp and perfectly tailored for a high-level admin role.
